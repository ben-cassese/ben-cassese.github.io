About Me

Before undergraduate work, I grew up in Rhode Island in a dog-filled home south of Providence. I switched coasts for college and studied Planetary Science and History at Caltech, eventually double-majoring in both (my B.S. in History will forever be an icebreaker fun fact). While in college, I was involved in student government and a cappella. My history thesis focused on federal litigation of partisan gerrymandering

I got the chance to blend my interests in science and public policy during a summer internship at the National Academy of Sciences in DC. There, I assisted on the Decadal Survey, and I've since stayed involved in science policy issues- I currently serve on the AAS DPS Federal Relations Subcommittee .

After finishing college, I took a year off to continue research on the Galilean satellites, volunteer for a local political campaign, and intern with the Rhode Island State General Assembly.

I'll start at Columbia University in the Astronomy PhD program in the Fall of 2021.

Part of my time between undergrad and graduate work was spent on the trail.
